A series of oil paintings exploring domestic objects under natural light. Each composition draws from the quiet intimacy of everyday items: ceramic vessels, glass, cloth. Arranged and rearranged until the light tells you where to look.

The work began as a study in translucency and evolved into an inquiry into the domestic still life as a site of attention. What does it mean to slow down and look?
