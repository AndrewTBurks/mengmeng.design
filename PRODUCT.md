# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

**Potential employers and freelance clients** visiting to evaluate creative work. They arrive with limited time and a critical eye, looking for evidence of craft, original thought, and someone worth working with. The site must earn trust and convey personality fast.

## Product Purpose

A personal portfolio at mengmeng.design that functions as the primary evidence of creative capability. It must communicate: "this person makes work worth noticing." The portfolio itself IS the work — every design decision reflects the designer's taste.

## Brand Personality

**Creative. Unique. Warm-happy.**

The work (and the site around it) carries personality that feels human and lived-in, not portfolio-generic. Warmth signals that collaboration would be good; uniqueness signals the work is worth looking at. The dog-energy: playful but not childish, approachable but not safe.

## Anti-references

**What this must NOT look like:**

- Behance-grid sameness: uniform card layouts, hover-reveal interactions everyone uses, the same three-section hero-about-skills structure
- Hero-reel template: full-screen video loops with minimal type as a substitute for curation
- The mile-wide portfolio: "I do branding, UI, motion, illustration, art direction, 3D, photography..."
- Cold-conceptual: work so self-serious it feels like it was made for a design award rather than a client
- Portfolio as resume: sites that lead with "about me" before showing work

## Design Principles

1. **Let the work lead.** The site exists to present work, not to perform the designer's personality. Personality lives in the curation and the details, not in oversized type statements.

2. **Craft in the margins.** The quality of a designer shows in the decisions nobody talks about: timing of transitions, whitespace rhythm, typographic detail. Make those count.

3. **Warmth without cuteness.** Happy and playful doesn't mean rounded fonts, bouncy animations, or dog illustrations on every page. The warmth is in the approachability of the experience.

4. **Specificity over breadth.** Showing less work, better presented, beats a comprehensive reel. Let the portfolio feel curated, not accumulated.

5. **Unique is a process, not a style.** Avoid the obvious by thinking one step deeper on every decision — not "what would a designer do" but "why would a designer do that, and is there a better reason."

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Default to WCAG AA. Reduced motion should be respected. The site should work across devices and browsers. No specific known user accommodations to account for yet.

- **Fonts:** Google Fonts (DM Mono)
- **Colors:** OKLCH throughout
- **Build:** `npm run build` → `dist/` (static HTML/CSS)
